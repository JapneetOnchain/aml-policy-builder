# Limitations and Honest Disclosure

## What this tool does well

- Generates a structured starting draft anchored to the January 8, 2026 FIU-IND Guidelines
- Every paragraph citation traces to actual text in the source document
- Calibrates output based on 19 inputs driving 14 conditional blocks across 7 modules
- Includes Statutory Appendix with verbatim text of 10 PMLA and PML Rules provisions
- Open source, inspectable, welcomes correction

## What this tool does NOT do

**1. Not legal advice.** The author is not a lawyer. Use does not create attorney-client relationship.

**2. Does not capture firm-specific operational details.** Generic framework based on broad firm profile. Must be customised after generation.

**3. Operational expansions vs verbatim regulatory text.** Some content goes beyond literal 2026 Guidelines text, adding operational detail consistent with FATF guidance and Indian banking AML practice. These are clearly identifiable (no paragraph citations from the 2026 Guidelines attached). They represent professional judgment, not regulatory fabrication.

**4. Statutory text reproduction.** Section 18 (Statutory Appendix) reproduces verbatim text from FIU-IND notifications page and Indian Kanoon. Users should verify against indiacode.nic.in before relying on text in any formal context.

**5. Regulatory currency.** Built on January 8, 2026 Guidelines. Users should verify current state of FIU-IND guidance before relying on output.

**6. Adjacent frameworks not fully integrated.** OECD CARF, Section 509 IT Act, CERT-In Directions referenced but not operationally expanded.

**7. Single-jurisdiction scope.** Indian VDA SPs under PMLA only. Not for Singapore, UAE, Hong Kong, US, EU.

**8. Business model scope.** Primarily calibrated for exchanges (spot, custody, OTC). Other models at higher abstraction.
